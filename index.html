Key Concepts
The 7 Subproducts
Strassen's formulas use P₁–P₇ which are cleverly designed so that only 7 scalar multiplications are needed to produce the four output quadrants — down from 8 in the naive approach.
Recurrence & Complexity
T(n) = 7T(n/2) + O(n²) — by the Master Theorem, this solves to O(nlog₂7) ≈ O(n²·⁸⁰⁷), compared to O(n³) for naive.
Parallelism
P₁ through P₇ are independent — they share no data dependencies. All seven can be computed simultaneously on parallel hardware, giving O(log²n) parallel depth.
Practical Notes
Constant factors and cache effects mean Strassen is only faster in practice for n ≳ 64–128. For non-power-of-2 sizes, the matrix is padded. Modern records: Coppersmith-Winograd → O(n²·³⁷³).
Independence of Subproblems
The 7 recursive subproblems operate on non-overlapping linear combinations of A and B quadrants. No subproblem depends on another's output — enabling both recursion and parallelism.
